# Cambridge Style English Exam: Phrasal Verbs

**Topic:** Phrasal Verbs in Context  
**Level:** B2 (Advanced)  
**Time:** 45 minutes

---

## Exercise 1: Multiple Choice Cloze

*Read the sentences below and choose the correct phrasal verb to complete the gap. Mark the correct letter (A, B, C, or D).*

1. I simply cannot _______ that terrible noise any longer!
   A) put up with  
   B) get down to  
   C) look in on  
   D) come up with

2. We need to _______ early tomorrow morning to avoid the traffic.
   A) set up  
   B) set off  
   C) take off  
   D) get by

3. The meeting has been _______ until next Tuesday due to the manager's illness.
   A) put out  
   B) called in  
   C) put off  
   D) brought up

4. I didn't mean to _______ this topic, but we need to discuss the budget.
   A) bring up  
   B) bring about  
   C) turn out  
   D) run into

5. She was offered the job, but she decided to _______ it _______.
   A) turn ... down  
   B) give ... back  
   C) put ... out  
   D) take ... over

## Exercise 2: Open Cloze (Text Completion)

*Read the text below and think of the word which best fits each gap. Use only **one** word in each gap. The missing word is usually the particle (preposition or adverb) of a phrasal verb.*

### **A New Hobby**

After years of working hard, John decided it was time to take (1) _______ a new hobby. He had always been interested in photography, so he signed (2) _______ for a local evening class. At first, he found it difficult to keep (3) _______ with the other students, as the technical terms were confusing. However, he didn't give (4) _______ easily. He spent hours reading manual books and looking (5) _______ words he didn't understand. Eventually, his hard work paid off, and his photos turned (6) _______ to be excellent.

## Exercise 3: Key Word Transformation

*Complete the second sentence so that it has a similar meaning to the first sentence, using the word given. **Do not change the word given**. You must use between **two** and **five** words, including the word given.*

1. **RESPECT**
   I really admire my grandfather for everything he has achieved.  
   I really _________________________________ my grandfather for everything he has achieved.

2. **RECOVER**
   It took Sarah a long time to recover from the flu.  
   It took Sarah a long time to _________________________________ the flu.

3. **INVESTIGATE**
   The police are investigating the burglary.  
   The police are _________________________________ the burglary.

4. **ABOLISH**
   They want to abolish the old laws regarding tax.  
   They want to _________________________________ the old laws regarding tax.

5. **OCCURRED**
   A great idea suddenly came to me while I was in the shower.  
   I _________________________________ a great idea while I was in the shower.

# Answer Key: Phrasal Verbs Exam

## Exercise 1: Multiple Choice Cloze

1. **A) put up with** (to tolerate)
2. **B) set off** (to start a journey)
3. **C) put off** (to postpone)
4. **A) bring up** (to mention a topic)
5. **A) turn ... down** (to reject/refuse)

## Exercise 2: Open Cloze

1. **up** (take up: to start a hobby)
2. **up** (sign up: to register)
3. **up** (keep up with: to move at the same speed/understand)
4. **up** (give up: to quit)
5. **up** (look up: to search for information)
6. **out** (turn out: to result/happen in the end)

## Exercise 3: Key Word Transformation

1. **look up to** (admire -> look up to)
2. **get over** (recover from -> get over)
3. **looking into** (investigating -> looking into)
4. **do away with** (abolish -> do away with)
5. **came up with** (occurred to me -> came up with)

## Exercise 4: Gap Fill with Word Bank

1. **run out of** (have none left)
2. **came across** (found by chance)
3. **broke down** (stopped working - mechanical)
4. **carry on** (continue)
5. **look forward to** (wait for with excitement)
*(Note: "fall out" and "get away with" were not used)*
